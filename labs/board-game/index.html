Part 1 - Class Construction

The game I chose for this assignment is Checkers. In the game Checkers, two players use two different sets of colored game pieces on a 64 grid board and attempt to eliminate the other player. The data needed for this game are the position of pieces on the board, player movement, total pieces left on the board, and a way to check which pieces have been eliminated. The main classes in this game are the red and black checker pieces and the board itself. The board keeps track of where each piece can move and how many pieces are currently on the board. The checkers share most of the same properites except that they are against each other. If one players piece hops over another then the opposing player loses their piece. Also, if the player reaches the opposing side of the board, they are able to "king" there piece, giving them an extra piece atop of the current one.
